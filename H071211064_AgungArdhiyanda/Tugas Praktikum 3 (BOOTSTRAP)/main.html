ABOUT US

Lockheed Martin Corporation is an aerospace, weapons, defense, security and advanced technology company from the United States (US). The largest supplier of systems and information technology to the US military is one of the world's largest companies according to Fortune's Global 500 records. Lockheed Martin operates in four business segments, namely Aeronautics, Missiles and Fire Control (MFC), Rotary and Mission Systems (RMS), and Space. The company's multiple business segments have sent Lockheed Martin skyrocketing in revenue and profit.


Lockheed Martin's corporate roots date back to the early 20th century, followed by the story Glenn Martin. In 1905, young Martin tried to build and fly a glider first experimental. With some motivation from the results of his experiments, Martin soon then started a small airplane factory while working as a salesman for the Ford factory and Maxwell. The money from his work, Martin used to finance his airplane business. During this time he hired a man named Donald Douglas to help him develop new aircraft. Soon after, Douglas and Martin collaborated to produced a small flight trainer called the Model TT which was sold to the US Army and Dutch government.

During his travels, Martin produced thousands of aircraft for the Allied war effort, including the A-30 . Baltimore, B-26 and B-29 bombers, PBM Mariner flying boats, and amphibious air carriers Mars weighs 70 tons. Here are some of the products produced from World War I to the War World II. After the war ended, Martin continued to produce some of the aircraft that were still ordered by Army and Navy. In 1947 the company re-entered the aircraft market very competitive commercial model with the so-called M-202.


In late 1951 George M. Bunker and J. Bradford Wharton, Jr., were asked to take transfer of company management. As part of the refinancing plan, Glenn Martin was given a honorary chairmanship. In addition his 275,000 shares in the company are placed in trust voting. Martin resigned from his position at the company in May 1953, but stay as director of the company until his death. George Bunker succeeded Martin as president and chairman and direct the company for the next 20 years.
